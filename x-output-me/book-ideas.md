1. The wrong boardroom question
Angle: “How do we adopt AI?” vs “How do we lead an organisation that can absorb and act on AI?”

Prompt: Write a LinkedIn article for European CEOs and C-suite readers. Open with why “How do we adopt AI?” produces pilots without sustained impact. Argue for reframing around organisational absorption: strategy, governance, culture—not IT-only ownership. Tone: direct, evidence-leaning, peer advisor. End with one question the board should ask next quarter. ~900–1200 words, short paragraphs, optional bullet “signals you’re asking the wrong question.”

2. This is not another software rollout
Angle: Paradigm shift vs incremental mental model; cost of treating AI as a project.

Prompt: Write a LinkedIn article explaining why framing AI transformation as a software rollout is structurally misaligned with the scale of change (industrial-revolution-class shift). Address European leaders specifically: ambition is not the gap—the mental model is. Include practical consequences (cost structure, talent, competitive window). Tone: urgent but not alarmist. Close with what “leading the shift” looks like in behaviour, not slogans.

Use European governance norms where helpful (works councils, co-determination as context, not as excuse for delay). Actionable: three decisions only the CEO can credibly make.

Prompt: Write an explainer-style LinkedIn article defining the Agentic Organisation: work redesigned around human–AI collaboration; agents embedded in workflows, not only Q&A. Contrast with “AI tools on legacy process.” Mention the agentic employee mindset (orchestration vs pure task execution) without jargon overload. One simple diagram described in words is fine. CTA: one week experiment for leadership teams.

European examples optional (generic “multinational BU silos” is fine). Finish with what leaders should enforce so local wins ladder to enterprise outcomes.

Stress that the image is a shared language for the management team. Keep each layer to a tight paragraph plus one “CEO question” per layer. Avoid vendor hype; no tool list.
